Who am I?

Hi there! My name is Sharna Jahan Hossain. I was born and raised in Brooklyn, New York and proudly live there to this day. My parents are immigrants from a small country in Southern Asia called Bangladesh. My fondest memories are of my Summers in the village, getting my feet mucky and wet in the heavy rains. I am a huge advocate for girls' education for I have seen firsthand how disadvantaged the girls from my parents' homeland and elswhere in the world are when it comes to equal rights and autonomy.

I believe that technology can pave the way for girls (and boys) all over the world. I believe that education should be accessible to everyone. I hope to one day help create a future where this is possible, and I design to do this with computer science. An organization that I am a huge fan of is Girls Who Code, and I would like to do something like that but on a global scale. As our world rapidly becomes more technology-inclined, it is important that children all over the world do not get left behind.
